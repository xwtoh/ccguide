    back <

    At SMU, we recognize that learning from co-curricular involvement does not occur automatically; neither should learning be left to chance. Instead, the learning has to be carefully and intentionally designed.


    As such, we strive to render visible the learning outcomes from co-curricular activities through the deliberate curation and design of learning experiences that map to established learning outcomes.


    It is imperative hence, for all co-curricular activities – from student clubs to internships and community service projects - to have explicit learning objectives, which are in turn aligned to the Graduate Learning Outcomes (GLOs) of the University. The goal is so that each co-curricular experience becomes a purposeful journey for students.

    Figure 1: SMU's Graduate Learning Outcomes (GLOs). Note that for co-curricular activities, GLO1 (Disciplinary and multidisciplinary knowledge) will not be applicable.



    Figure 2: How Learning Outcomes (LOs) in an activity is linked to SMU's Graduate Learning Outcomes (GLOs). In this example, the activity addresses GLO2 (Intellectual and creartive skills) and GLO5 (Personal mastery).


    Anthology Portfolio is thus the platform of choice to record, document and assess students.


    Each co-curricular activity that a student is involved in is reflected as a single Placement entry within the student's Anthology Portfolio account (See Figure 3).


    Placement (CCA)


    Figure 3: Placement entry

    Every Placement will have a customised reflection journal based on the activity's Graduate Learning Outcomes (GLOs).

    Student submission of reflections for CCA/Competitions/Workshops is optional, but encouraged, as it provides an additional point of reference for assessment.

    The assessment of the student for the activity is done in the following order:

    1. CCA staff-in-charge (optional)
    2. CCD using the Transformative Learning Rubric (See Figure 2)


    Staff assessment in accordance to identified GLOs can be based on:

    1. Direct observation
    2. Feedback from others
    3. Student's reflection (if submitted)


    *Assessment by the CCA staff-in-charge is optional. Staff can use the Department Rubric to assess the student's level of attainment for the identified GLOs in the particular activity.

    For short activities such as workshops, there will be no Department Rubric, and students will be automatically graded as "Exposure" (akin to a "Participation" grade) on the Transformative Learning Rubric.

    Placement (CCA)


    Figure 2: Placement entry (with GLOs)

    Upon graduation, students will have a CCA Record generated based on records in Anthology Portfolio, which will eflect the achievement across the GLOs.

    As such, it is important that when planning and administering CCAs, the learning objectives of each CCA is mapped to the appropriate SMU GLOs.


    Identify GLOs

    back <


    • Identify the learning outcomes (based on SMU's Graduate Learning Outcomes) of the particular activity. This can be done in collaboration with the CCA mentor and/or CCA Exco, etc.

    • Learning outcomes are concise statements that describe what students are expected to know or be able to do upon completion.

    • Begin with a verb: Understanding, identifying, applying, analyzing, evaluating, creating.

    • The rest of the statement should describe the knowledge and abilities to be demonstrated. For example, applying Design thinking to address problems.

    • Realistically, each activity's should have LOs proportionate to the duration of the activity. A club's activities would possibly have more LOs than say, a two-day workshop. Of course there can be exceptions, but remember to be realistic when suggesting LOs.



    • Once the LOs for the particular activity has been decided, you will need to match the LOs to the SMU Graduate Learning Outcomes (GLOs).

    • This is important as the student will be assessed across all their experiences using these GLOs, as the specfic LOs for every activity will be unique, and the GLOs affords a point of comparison across the board.

    • This linking of LOs to GLOs should be intuitive. If in doubt, please feel free to consult CCD.

    • Your activity can have up to 4 GLOs (GLO 1: Disciplinary and Multidisciplinary Knowledge is address only in the academic domain and will not apply to CCAs).

    • Collate the different GLOs that your activity is linked to, and prepare to fill it in the template when creating CCA entries (addressed in Step 2: Creating CCA/Student entries).

    Creating entries

    back <

    Note: Please ensure that only students that have matriculated in 2021 and later are included.



    • Inform students participating in the activity the learning outcomes of the activity as identified in Step 1: Identify learning outcomes of the activity (mapped to SMU GLOs). This is to help make their participation in the activity purposeful and for learning to be intentional.

    • Inform students that they are encouraged to submit a reflection on Anthology Portfolio upon completion of the activity (when the Placement entry for the activity has been created). The reflection will be considered in the assessment of their level of attainment for the identified GLOs.



    • Within 6 weeks from the end of the activity, complete the Activity Template for Anthology Portfolio (downloadable below). Information captured includes details of the activity as well as the names of students involved and their roles.

      Note: Remember to included the identifed GLOs in the template, as addressed in Step 1: Identify learning outcomes of the activity (mapped to SMU GLOs).

    • Email the completed Activity Template for Anthology Portfolio to CCD here.

      Note: The Placement entries for the activity will be created in Anthology Portfolio within 5 working days from the date the "Activity Template for Anthology Portfolio" is received. The respective staff member will be notified when the Placement entries have been created.

    Searching

    back <


    • Once the student activity entry has been created, it will appear as a Placement within Anthology.

    • Go into Anthology Portfolio, click on Menu (on the top right of the screen)

    • Click on Experiential Learning, and there will be a list of students with ongoing internships/community service projects.

    • Click on Search Placements, and there will be a series of filters that can be input to help you find the placement or series of placement(s).


    • You can filter placements by Start Dates, Stakeholders, and even custom fields such as Admit Term.

      Note: To filter by custom fields, click on the Filter using custom fields dropdown.

      To include more/less fields in the Placements list (for example, end date is not included by default), click Include These Columns in Report and check the relevant fields (such as end-date).

    • Alternatively, there is also a search box on the right, where you can search for keywords such as student names within the placements.

    Viewing reflection

    • After 1 month from the creation of the placement entry (and about 7 weeks from the end date of the activity), the student should have completed their reflection.

    • Go into Anthology Portfolio, click on Menu (on the top right of the screen)

    • Click on Experiential Learning, and there will be a list of students with ongoing activities. Click on View.

    • If completed, the reflection can be found inside the Placement entry, as a Journal under the Journal heading.

    Sample roll-up

    Assessing


    • Read the Reflection Journal, then click Return. This might bring you to the Experiential Learning page, so you will need to click on the Placement you were going to assess earlier, and then View.

    • Under the Assessments header, click on <Department> Reflection Rubric , and Assess.

    Reporting

    back <


    • After the Placement has been closed, which can take up to 13 weeks from the end of the activity, a report can be generated to show aggregated student information.

    • For example, information such as the student performance across GLOs, or other assessment information can be exported as a report.

    • This information can be used to inform future projects or activities, to address areas that might be lacking in the co-curricular sphere.

    • Go into Anthology Portfolio, click on Menu (on the top right of the screen)

    • Click on Reporting, and the Student Results. Click on Result Search and a dropdown with different fields will appear.

    • Here, you may sort the student data depending on the report that you require. Students can be sorted by Assessment Instruments, Outcome, Assessor and other fields.
