    At SMU, we recognize that learning from co-curricular involvement does not occur automatically; neither should learning be left to chance. Instead, the learning has to be carefully and intentionally designed.


    As such, we strive to render visible the learning outcomes from co-curricular activities through the deliberate curation and design of learning experiences that map to established learning outcomes.


    It is imperative hence, for all co-curricular activities â€“ from student clubs to internships and community service projects - to have explicit learning objectives, which are in turn aligned to the Graduate Learning Outcomes (GLOs) of the University. The goal is so that each co-curricular experience becomes a purposeful journey for students.

    Figure 1: SMU's Graduate Learning Outcomes (GLOs). Note that for co-curricular activities, GLO1 (Disciplinary and multidisciplinary knowledge) will not be applicable.



    Figure 2: How Learning Outcomes (LOs) in an activity is linked to SMU's Graduate Learning Outcomes (GLOs). In this example, the activity addresses GLO2 (Intellectual and creartive skills) and GLO5 (Personal mastery).


    Anthology Portfolio is thus the platform of choice to record, document and assess students.


    Each co-curricular activity that a student is involved in is reflected as a single Placement entry within the student's Anthology Portfolio account (See Figure 3).


    Placement (CCA)


    Figure 3: Placement entry

    Every Placement will have a customised reflection journal based on the activity's Graduate Learning Outcomes (GLOs).

    Student submission of reflections for CCA/Competitions/Workshops is optional, but encouraged, as it provides an additional point of reference for assessment.

    The assessment of the student for the activity is done in the following order:

    1. CCA staff-in-charge (optional)
    2. CCD using the Transformative Learning Rubric (click here to download)


    Staff assessment in accordance to identified GLOs can be based on:

    1. Direct observation
    2. Feedback from mentors, coaches, peer leaders etc.
    3. Student's reflection (if submitted)


    *Assessment by the CCA staff-in-charge is optional. Staff can use the Department Rubric to assess the student's level of attainment for the identified GLOs in the particular activity.

    For short activities such as workshops, there will be no Department Rubric, and students will be automatically graded as "Exposure" (akin to a "Participation" grade) on the Transformative Learning Rubric.

    Placement (CCA)


    Figure 2: Placement entry (with GLOs)

    Upon graduation, students will have a CCA Record generated based on records in Anthology Portfolio, which will reflect the achievement across the GLOs.

    As such, it is important that when planning and administering CCAs, the learning objectives of each CCA is mapped to the appropriate SMU GLOs.


    Identify GLOs

    back <


    • Identify the Graduate Learning Outcome(s) (GLOs) that match(es) the learning outcomes of the co-curricular activity.
      This can be done in collaboration with the CCA mentor and/or CCA Exco, etc.

    • Indicate a minimum of 1 and a maximum of 4 GLOs

    • You can indicate GLOs 2-5. Choose GLOs that most students in the activity would have a chance to address.
      (GLO 1: Disciplinary and Multidisciplinary Knowledge will not apply to CCAs).

    • Indicate the chosen GLOs in the template in Step 2: Creating CCA/Student entries).

    Creating entries

    back <

    Note: Please only include students that have matriculated in 2021 and later.



    • Inform students participating in the activity the learning outcomes identified in Step 1: Identify GLOs. This is to help make their participation in the activity purposeful and for learning to be intentional.

    • Inform students that they are encouraged to submit a reflection on Anthology Portfolio when the Placement entry for the activity has been created (after the completion of the activity).

    • This reflection will be considered when assessing the impact of the learning experience on the students.



    • Complete the Activity Template for Anthology Portfolio (downloadable below) within 6 weeks after activity ends.

      Note: Remember to indicate the activity's GLOs in the template, as addressed in Step 1: Identify GLOs.

    • Email the completed Activity Template for Anthology Portfolio to CCD here.

      Note: The placements will be created in Anthology Portfolio within 1 week upon submission of the template. CCD will notify staff when the placements have been created.

    Searching

    back <


    • Student activity entries appear as placements within Anthology.

    • Go into Anthology Portfolio, click on Menu (on the top right of the screen)

    • Click on Experiential Learning, and there will be a list of student activities under your charge.

    • Click on Search Placements, and there will be a series of filters that can be input to help you find the placement or series of placement(s).


    • You can filter placements by Start Dates, Stakeholders, and even custom fields such as Admit Term.

      Note: To filter by custom fields, click on the Filter using custom fields dropdown.

      To include more/less fields in the Placements list (for example, end date is not included by default), click Include These Columns in Report and check the relevant fields (such as end-date).

    • Alternatively, there is also a search box on the right, where you can search for keywords such as student names.


    Assessing

    back <


    Viewing reflection

    • After 1 month from the creation of the placement entry (7 weeks from the end of the activity), the student should have completed their reflection.

    • Go into Anthology Portfolio, click on Menu (on the top right of the screen)

    • Click on Experiential Learning, and there will be a list of students with ongoing activities.

    • Click on View on the entry you wish to assess.

    • If completed, the reflection can be found inside the Placement entry, as a Journal under the Journal heading.

    Assessing


    • Read the Reflection Journal, then click Return. This might bring you to the Experiential Learning page, so you will need to click on the Placement you were going to assess earlier, and then View.

    • Under the Assessments header, click on <Department> Reflection Rubric , and Assess.

    Reporting

    back <


    • After the placement has been closed by CCD, a report can be generated.

    • For example, information such as the student performance across GLOs, or other assessment information can be reported.

    • The information might then inform future projects or activities, or address areas that might be lacking.

    • Go into Anthology Portfolio, click on Menu (on the top right of the screen)

    • Click on Reporting, and the Student Results. Click on Result Search and a dropdown with different fields will appear.

    • Here, you may sort the student data depending on the report that you require. Students can be sorted by Assessment Instruments, Outcome, Assessor and other fields.
