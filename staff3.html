Co-curricular Activities *

back <

* Note: This section of the guide does not apply to students/managers under the Office of Student Life (OSL). Please approach OSL for more information and the steps required.

Anthology Portfolio is the platform where a student's co-curricular activities are documented and assessed.

Each co-curricular activity that a student is involved in is reflected as a single Placement entry within the student's Anthology Portfolio account (See Figure 1).


Placement (CCA)


Figure 1: Placement entry (student view)

Every Placement will have a reflection journal created. The reflection journal is customised based on the SMU Graduate Learning Outcomes (GLOs) earlier identified for the activity (i.e. GLO 2, GLO 3, GLO 4 and/or GLO 5). Student submission of reflections for CCA/Competitions/Workshops is optional, but encouraged. If submitted, the student's reflection provides an additional point of reference for assessing the student's level of attainment for the identified GLOs in the particular activity.

The student's impact of learning in the particular activity is assessed by the CCA staff-in-charge using the Department Rubric* (optional) and finally by CCD using the Transformative Learning Rubric (See Figure 2). Staff assessment of the student's level of attainment for the identified GLOs in the particular activity can be based on observation, feedback and/or the student's reflection (if submitted).

*Assessment by the CCA staff-in-charge is optional. Staff can use the Department Rubric to assess the student's level of attainment for the identified GLOs in the particular activity.

For short activities such as workshops, there will be no Department Rubric, and students will be automatically graded as "Exposure" (akin to a "Participation" grade) on the Transformative Learning Rubric.

Placement (CCA)


Figure 12: Placement entry (staff view)

At the end of their undergraduate years, each student will have a CCA Record that will be generated based on records in Anthology Portfolio. The CCA Record will reflect the student's attainment level of the SMU GLOs which each CCA is mapped to. As such, it is important that when planning and administering CCAs, the learning objectives of each CCA is mapped to the appropriate SMU GLOs.

Creating entries

back <

Note: Please ensure that only students that have matriculated in 2021 and later are included.



  • Identify the learning outcomes (based on SMU's Graduate Learning Outcomes) of the particular activity. This can be done in collaboration with the CCA mentor and/or CCA Exco, etc.

  • Inform students participating in the activity the learning outcomes of the activity. This is to help make their participation in the activity purposeful and for learning to be intentional.

  • Inform students that they are encouraged to submit a reflection on Anthology Portfolio upon completion of the activity (when the Placement entry for the activity has been created). The reflection will be considered in the assessment of their level of attainment for the identified GLOs.



  • Within 6 weeks from the end of the activity, complete the Activity Template for Anthology Portfolio (downloadable below). Information captured includes details of the activity as well as the names of students involved and their roles.

  • Email the completed Activity Template for Anthology Portfolio to CCD here.

    Note: The Placement entries for the activity will be created in Anthology Portfolio within 5 working days from the date the "Activity Template for Anthology Portfolio" is received. The respective staff member will be notified when the Placement entries have been created.

Searching

back <


  • Once the student activity entry has been created, it will appear as a Placement within Anthology.

  • Go into Anthology Portfolio, click on Menu (on the top right of the screen)

  • Click on Experiential Learning, and there will be a list of students with ongoing internships/community service projects.

  • Click on Search Placements, and there will be a series of filters that can be input to help you find the placement or series of placement(s).


  • You can filter placements by Start Dates, Stakeholders, and even custom fields such as Admit Term.

    Note: To filter by custom fields, click on the Filter using custom fields dropdown.

    To include more/less fields in the Placements list (for example, end date is not included by default), click Include These Columns in Report and check the relevant fields (such as end-date).

  • Alternatively, there is also a search box on the right, where you can search for keywords such as student names within the placements.


Assessing

back <


Viewing reflection

  • After 1 month from the creation of the placement entry (and about 7 weeks from the end date of the activity), the student should have completed their reflection.

  • Go into Anthology Portfolio, click on Menu (on the top right of the screen)

  • Click on Experiential Learning, and there will be a list of students with ongoing activities. Click on View.

  • If completed, the reflection can be found inside the Placement entry, as a Journal under the Journal heading.

Sample roll-up

Assessing


  • Read the Reflection Journal, then click Return. This might bring you to the Experiential Learning page, so you will need to click on the Placement you were going to assess earlier, and then View.

  • Under the Assessments header, click on <Department> Reflection Rubric , and Assess.

Reporting

back <


  • After the Placement has been closed, which can take up to 13 weeks from the end of the activity, a report can be generated to show aggregated student information.

  • For example, information such as the student performance across GLOs, or other assessment information can be exported as a report.

  • This information can be used to inform future projects or activities, to address areas that might be lacking in the co-curricular sphere.

  • Go into Anthology Portfolio, click on Menu (on the top right of the screen)

  • Click on Reporting, and the Student Results. Click on Result Search and a dropdown with different fields will appear.

  • Here, you may sort the student data depending on the report that you require. Students can be sorted by Assessment Instruments, Outcome, Assessor and other fields.
